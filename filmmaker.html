“Water Can Float a Boat, Water Can Sink a Boat” 2013

This short was the product of a long seated ambition I had in high school to make a substantive movie under the guise of a school project. Finally, a project came along in my Chinese class, and I talked my teacher into letting me get very ambitious. This five minute movie ended up taking three months to complete. I worked on my own to animate tens of different shots, lip-sync thousands of frames, and to write the movie.

The project was in fact a group one, and originally I had my five groupmates cast for a live-action piece. But soon I realized how resistant they were to memorize lines or set aside their lunch hour, and decided I had to take more responsibility for my own passion project. With the help of one classmate in recording vocal performances, and the others in translating, I was able to focus all of my energy rotating through the various tasks requisite to creating the movie. What resulted is at least a charming and entertaining effort, borne of and constanty demanding an industrious attitude.



I learned what the demands of a short film project were by making a bunch of them. I have a library of attempts both laughable and compelling. Below are four which fall nearer the "compelling" end of the spectrum. If any titles do pique your curiousity, I would suggest that you click on them, and let them wait in their respective tabs while you review the rest of my portfolio.

THE FINAL SMORGAS the final, and most polished in a series of "dysfunctional cooking show" videos I made with my friend Janie. This entry also served as a companion to our graduation party invitations.
